# SaaS GTM Channel Patterns

## Community-Led Channels
- **LinkedIn Groups & Posts**: Best for B2B SaaS targeting PMs, Support Leads, and Operations roles. Founder-led content (build-in-public, data insights, case studies) outperforms ads at early stage. Typical reach: 500–5,000 impressions per post for a 500-connection account.
- **Slack Communities**: High-intent, niche B2B audiences. Examples: Rands Leadership Slack, Support Driven, Product Led Growth Slack, RevGenius, Demand Curve. Best for direct DMs and sharing resources, not broadcasting.
- **Reddit**: r/SaaS, r/startups, r/CustomerSuccess, r/ProductManagement. Works for authentic problem-solution posts. No direct pitching — post insights and link to tools only in comments when relevant.
- **Discord Servers**: Developer-focused products. Examples: Buildspace, Indie Hackers Discord, specific tool communities (e.g., Notion, Linear).
- **SaaStr / Indie Hackers / Product Hunt**: High-visibility launches and AMAs. Product Hunt launch best for initial spike; Indie Hackers for ongoing community engagement.

## Outbound Channels
- **Cold Email (Personalized)**: Effective at MVP/early-revenue stage with tight ICP. Target: 50–200 sends/week, >30% open rate, >5% reply rate as benchmark. Use Apollo.io or Clay for prospecting. Subject line should reference a specific pain, not the product.
- **LinkedIn DMs (Sales Navigator)**: Best for high-ACV ($500+/month) products. Warm with content engagement before outreach. Connection request + 2-message sequence max before moving on.
- **Cold Calling**: Rarely effective for SaaS < $10K ACV. Reserve for enterprise sales.

## Content & SEO Channels
- **Founder Blog / SEO**: Long-term compounding channel. Target long-tail keywords (e.g., "how to analyze support tickets automatically"). Minimum 3–6 months before organic traffic meaningful.
- **YouTube / Video**: High-trust channel for complex SaaS products. Product walkthroughs, tutorials, and "how I built X" content. 6–12 month timeline for traction.
- **Guest Posts & Podcast Appearances**: Borrow existing audiences. Target publications your ICP reads: First Round Review, SaaStr Blog, Support Driven Blog, Lenny's Newsletter.
- **Newsletter Sponsorships**: Cost-effective for B2B niche audiences. Examples: Lenny's Newsletter, The Hustle, TLDR for developers.

## Product-Led Channels
- **Free Tool / Freemium**: Acquires users via utility. Works best when the free version demonstrates the core value and creates a natural upgrade trigger.
- **Integration Marketplace Listings**: List on Zendesk Marketplace, HubSpot App Directory, Intercom App Store, Notion Gallery. High intent — users searching for solutions.
- **API / Open Source**: Developer-led growth. Publish an SDK, snippet, or open-source component. GitHub stars and forks signal interest.

## Partnership Channels
- **Technology Partners**: Co-marketing with complementary tools (e.g., a support ticket analysis tool partners with Zendesk consultants or Intercom agencies).
- **Affiliate / Referral Programs**: Works after product-market fit. Benchmark: 20–30% revenue share for B2B SaaS.
- **Agency Partnerships**: Productize via agencies that serve your ICP. Agencies get a margin; you get distribution.

## Paid Channels (Medium/High Budget Only)
- **LinkedIn Ads**: Expensive ($8–$15 CPC) but precise B2B targeting. Minimum $3,000/month to get meaningful data.
- **Google Search Ads**: Best for products with clear search intent. Bid on competitor names + pain-point keywords.
- **Retargeting**: Only effective once you have 500+ monthly visitors. Use for demo request conversion.

## Channel Selection by Stage & Budget
| Stage | Budget | Top 3 Channels |
|-------|--------|----------------|
| Idea / MVP | Low | Cold email, LinkedIn DMs, Slack communities |
| MVP | Low-Medium | LinkedIn content, SaaS forums, integration listings |
| Early Revenue | Medium | SEO/blog, Product Hunt, partnership outreach |
| Growth | High | Paid LinkedIn, Google Ads, affiliate program |
